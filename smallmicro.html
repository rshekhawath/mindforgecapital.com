The strategy

What it does

The MultiFactor SmallMicro 500 strategy covers the combined universe of the Nifty Smallcap 250 and Nifty Microcap 250 indices — 500 stocks spanning the smallest and least-researched segment of listed Indian equities. From this universe, a systematic multi-factor model selects the top 50 stocks each month, capped at 3 per industry.

Small and microcap stocks are genuinely different from large-cap stocks. They are thinner, more volatile, more sensitive to liquidity conditions, and more prone to sharp momentum crashes. A model designed for large-cap stocks applied naively to this segment would fail. The SmallMicro strategy uses a factor model purpose-built for these dynamics.

The universe of 500 stocks covers India's broadest listed segment by company count — from established midcap graduates that recently crossed into smallcap territory, to genuinely micro-scale companies with high growth potential and correspondingly high risk. The combination of both Smallcap 250 and Microcap 250 provides both breadth and depth.

This is MindForge's most aggressive strategy, with the highest expected return and highest risk profile. Its recommended investment of ₹25,00,000 is sized to achieve meaningful diversification across 50 equal-weight positions.

Why a separate model for small and micro cap? Thin liquidity means price moves without volume are illiquid spikes that immediately reverse. The model explicitly checks that price strength is accompanied by rising trading volume — a crucial filter that does not matter in large-cap but is essential here.
Max drawdown: -28.0% in the 5-year backtest — higher than the LargeMidcap strategy, as expected given the more volatile universe. The Sharpe ratio of 1.46 suggests this additional risk has historically been more than compensated by additional return.
Retail-dominated markets. Small and microcap stocks are disproportionately traded by individual investors, creating stronger behavioural inefficiencies — particularly around momentum and 52-week highs — that a systematic model can systematically exploit.
Why small & micro cap?

The opportunity and the challenge

Academic research going back to Fama and French's 1992 work consistently documents a size premium — small-cap stocks have historically delivered higher returns than large-cap stocks. From 1927 to 2015, the U.S. size premium averaged 3.3% per year. In India, the Nifty Smallcap 250 has historically delivered superior long-run returns compared to large-cap indices, though with significantly higher volatility.

The reason for this premium is well understood: smaller companies carry greater economic cycle risk, more limited access to capital, higher earnings volatility, and less analyst coverage. Investors demand a higher expected return to bear these risks. The premium is compensation for genuine risk, not a free lunch.

However, capturing this premium naively — simply by owning an equal-weight basket of all smallcap stocks — is not optimal. A well-constructed factor model can identify which small and microcap stocks are most likely to outperform in the near term and which are likely to underperform, dramatically improving the risk-return profile of the exposure.

The challenge of the small and microcap segment is real. Liquidity can dry up quickly. A stock that looks strong on price may have zero buyers two days later. Corporate governance standards vary enormously. Information is scarcer and less reliable. Many companies in this segment are genuinely high-risk businesses.

The SmallMicro strategy addresses these challenges directly. The factor model incorporates an explicit volume signal to ensure that price strength is real and liquid, not a thin-market illusion. An industry cap of 3 stocks prevents any single sector blow-up from devastating the portfolio. And monthly rebalancing means that deteriorating stocks are removed quickly rather than held indefinitely.

The result is a 50-stock portfolio that aims to capture the small and microcap premium while systematically managing the specific risks that make naive exposure to this segment dangerous.

Momentum with volume confirmation

Price momentum is a core signal, but only when supported by rising trading volume. Illiquid price spikes — common in microcap — are filtered out.

Trend strength filter

A trend confirmation signal acts as a circuit breaker on momentum runs built on fragile foundations — especially important in small-cap where mean-reversion after momentum crashes is sharper.

52-week high proximity

Retail-dominated small-cap markets show stronger anchoring bias. Stocks near their 52-week highs attract buying pressure more aggressively than in large-cap, making this signal especially predictive here.

Relative volatility regime

In small-cap, very low absolute volatility often signals a frozen or illiquid stock — the opposite of what it means in large-cap. The model uses short-term vs long-term volatility ratio instead, which is a far more informative signal.

Industry cap for protection

A hard limit of 3 stocks per industry ensures that a sector-specific event — a regulatory shock, a commodity crash — cannot wipe out a disproportionate share of the portfolio.

Fresh NSE data every cycle

Both the Nifty Smallcap 250 and Microcap 250 constituent lists are fetched directly from NSE at every rebalance — the model always reflects current index composition.

Important considerations

This strategy is not for everyone

The SmallMicro strategy carries the highest risk profile of MindForge's three strategies. A maximum drawdown of -28.0% in the backtest means that at some points, the portfolio would have declined by more than a quarter of its value before recovering. Investors must be genuinely comfortable with this level of volatility.

The recommended investment of ₹25,00,000 is set deliberately — with 50 equal-weight positions, this equates to ₹50,000 per stock, which is the minimum needed to trade most smallcap stocks without incurring disproportionate transaction costs or market impact.

Factor strategies in the small and microcap segment can go through extended periods of underperformance relative to a passive smallcap index. The model does not predict these periods — it simply continues executing its process. Investors who will panic-sell or deviate from the model during drawdowns should not use this strategy.

This strategy is appropriate for sophisticated investors with a long time horizon (minimum 3–5 years), a genuine understanding of factor investing, and the financial and emotional capacity to hold through volatility.
