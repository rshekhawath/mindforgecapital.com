How this is calculated
Both portfolios start at your initial investment and grow at the same expected annual return, compounded once a year. The only variable is how the fee is charged:
  • Traditional mutual fund (Scenario A): the AUM fee is a percentage skimmed off the return every year, so the portfolio compounds at return − AUM fee. Because it's a percentage of your whole corpus, the rupee cost grows as you do.
  • MindForge flat fee (Scenario B): the portfolio compounds at the full return, then a fixed annual amount (monthly fee × 12) is deducted at the end of each year. The fee never scales with your corpus.
"Wealth saved by switching" is simply the difference between the two final corpuses. On smaller portfolios with a low AUM fee, a flat fee can occasionally cost more — the calculator shows that honestly.
