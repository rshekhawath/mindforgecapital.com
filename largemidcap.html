The strategy

What it does

The MultiFactor LargeMidcap 250 strategy applies a rigorous quantitative scoring model to every stock in the Nifty LargeMidcap 250 index each month. All 250 stocks are evaluated and ranked. The top 25 — subject to a maximum of 3 stocks per industry — are selected and held in equal weight until the next rebalance.

The Nifty LargeMidcap 250 index covers the 100 largest companies on NSE plus 150 midcap stocks — a universe that captures the majority of India's listed market cap while still offering significant alpha opportunities compared to a pure large-cap strategy.

The 3-per-industry cap ensures the portfolio never becomes excessively concentrated in any single sector. Equal weighting means no single stock dominates the portfolio's returns — every holding has the same opportunity to contribute.

The model is rebuilt from scratch each month using fresh price and volume data. There are no overrides, no exceptions, and no subjective judgements. Stocks enter and exit purely based on their quantitative score.

The Nifty LargeMidcap 250 universe covers India's most liquid, well-researched listed companies across large and midcap segments. It provides breadth — 250 stocks across 19 industries — while staying in the zone where price discovery is efficient and data is reliable.
Maximum drawdown: -20.9% in the 5-year backtest versus -19.3% for the equal-weight benchmark. The multi-factor scoring naturally tilts away from stocks in deteriorating trends, providing some downside protection versus a passive index approach.
Beat the benchmark in approximately 64% of monthly periods over the 5-year backtest window — consistent outperformance, not just a few lucky years.
Strategy pillars

How it generates alpha

Price momentum

The model rewards stocks that have delivered strong returns over recent months — an academically documented anomaly that holds across every major equity market in the world. Stocks in motion tend to stay in motion.

Trend confirmation

Momentum alone can be noisy. A trend-strength signal filters out short-term spikes and confirms that price strength is sustained and directional — not a one-week spike that immediately reverses.

Sector diversification

A hard cap of 3 stocks per industry prevents any single sector from dominating the portfolio. Even when one industry is leading, the portfolio maintains meaningful exposure across multiple sectors.

Volatility awareness

All else being equal, the model prefers stocks with lower realised volatility. This is consistent with the well-documented low-volatility anomaly — lower-risk stocks frequently outperform on a risk-adjusted basis.

Liquidity filter

An illiquidity penalty discourages stocks with large price impact relative to their trading volume. This keeps the portfolio concentrated in names where entries and exits can be executed cleanly at realistic prices.

Fresh data every month

The Nifty LargeMidcap 250 constituent list is fetched directly from NSE at every rebalance. The model always reflects the current index composition, including any additions or removals since the last cycle.
